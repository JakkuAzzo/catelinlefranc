Box Camera E20 2¼" B Film | Vintage Camera Experimentations

About This Camera

The Houghton Butcher Ensign Box Camera from 1927 represents a pivotal moment in accessible photography. This simple yet elegant design brought photography to the masses, allowing everyday people to capture their world with a device that required minimal technical knowledge.

Working with this nearly century-old camera presents unique challenges and opportunities. The soft focus, light leaks, and unpredictable nature of the aging mechanism create images with a dreamlike quality that cannot be replicated with modern equipment. Each photograph becomes a collaboration between photographer, camera, and time itself.

This series explores how this vintage technology interprets contemporary subjects, bridging the gap between past and present through the medium of light and chemistry.
