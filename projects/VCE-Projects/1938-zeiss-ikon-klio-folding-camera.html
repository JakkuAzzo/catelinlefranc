Medium Format Folding | Vintage Camera Experimentations

About This Camera

These images are taken with my 1938 Zeiss Ikon Klio folding camera. This camera uses 120 roll film and represents late pre-war European craftsmanship in portable medium format photography.

The Klio's folding bellows design makes it compact while still delivering generous negative size. Its fixed-focus simplicity and mechanical reliability encourage deliberate shooting and an appreciation for light and timing.

Images from this camera often present subtle edge falloff, gentle contrast, and a timeless rendering that pairs beautifully with modern subjects—creating a visual dialogue between eras.
